## Hi there 👋

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at National Taiwan University, specializing in operating system design for emerging storage devices. My recent research on optimizing the B-tree filesystem (Btrfs) for zoned namespace SSDs has been accepted to ASP-DAC 2026, a leading international conference in design automation and systems research (Qualis A2, <30% acceptance rate).

From 2016 to 2022, I worked at Synology as a Product Developer in the Filesystem R&D team, where I enhanced enterprise storage reliability by designing and implementing Btrfs applications, including Snapshot Replication - a flagship feature widely adopted in enterprise NAS solutions. I also led the development of a petabyte-scale file system for high-density storage, gaining hands-on experience in building scalable, production-grade systems through close collaboration with cross-functional teams.

My research explores the intersection of operating systems and storage, with a focus on file system design for emerging storage technologies. I seek to develop system software principles that not only address scalability, reliability, and performance challenges but also inform the design of future storage systems.
